Early Life and Education

Easterine Kire was born on March 29, 1959, in Kohima, Nagaland, into the Angami Naga community. Growing up in a region shaped by cultural richness and conflict, she developed a deep connection to Naga oral traditions. She earned a Ph.D. in English Literature from the University of Pune, which influenced her literary career.

Political Career

Easterine Kire has not been involved in formal politics but has significantly contributed to socio-cultural discourse through her writings. Her novel Bitter Wormwood explores the Naga conflict, advocating for understanding and reconciliation. Her work promotes Naga identity and heritage on national and international platforms.
