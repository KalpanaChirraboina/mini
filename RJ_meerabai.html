Meerabai, a 16th-century mystic poet and devotee from Rajasthan, is renowned for her devotional bhakti poetry dedicated to Lord Krishna. Born into a royal Rajput family, she defied societal norms to pursue a spiritual life, composing soulful verses in Hindi and Rajasthani that express her intense love for Krishna. Her poetry, blending simplicity and profound devotion, made her a central figure in the Bhakti movement, inspiring millions across centuries.

Early Life and Education

Meerabai was born around 1498 in Kudki, Rajasthan, into the Rathore Rajput clan. Her father, Ratan Singh, was a noble in the kingdom of Merta. Raised in a devout Vaishnava family, she developed an early devotion to Lord Krishna, learning music, poetry, and scriptures. Legends say she considered Krishna her divine husband from childhood, shaping her spiritual journey.

Personal Life and Children

Meerabai married Bhojraj, prince of Mewar, in an arranged marriage around 1516. After his death in 1521, she rejected royal life, dedicating herself to Krishna. She had no children and faced persecution from her in-laws for her devotion. Her life as a wandering mystic defined her personal journey, centered on spiritual love.
