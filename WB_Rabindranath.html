Early Life and Education

Rabindranath Tagore was born on May 7, 1861, in Kolkata to a prominent Brahmo family. The youngest of thirteen children, he was home-schooled in literature, music, and arts. He briefly studied law at University College London but returned to India without a degree, immersing himself in writing and managing family estates, which inspired his deep connection to rural Bengal.

Personal Life and Children

Tagore married Mrinalini Devi in 1883 and had five children: Rathindranath, Madhurilata, Renuka, Meera, and Shamindranath. Widowed in 1902, he raised his children while managing family estates and writing. His travels across Europe, Asia, and America enriched his worldview, and he lived a life dedicated to creativity, education, and social reform.
