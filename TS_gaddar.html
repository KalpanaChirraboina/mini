Gummadi Vittal Rao, popularly known as Gaddar, was a revolutionary Telugu poet, balladeer, and activist from Telangana, celebrated as the voice of the marginalized. His powerful songs and performances, rooted in folk traditions, mobilized masses for social justice, the Naxalite movement, and Telangana statehood. Founder of Jana Natya Mandali, Gaddar's work blended Marxist and Ambedkarite ideologies, addressing caste oppression and labor exploitation, leaving an indelible mark on Telugu culture and activism.
