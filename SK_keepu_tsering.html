Keepu Tsering Lepcha, known as the Jewel of Sikkim, is a distinguished social worker, educationist, and former civil servant who founded the Human Development Foundation of Sikkim (HDFS). Her lifelong dedication to uplifting the Lepcha community, particularly through education and cultural preservation, has earned her accolades such as the Padma Shri in 2009. By establishing Lepcha Cottage and running a school for underprivileged children, she has provided hope and opportunities to countless orphans and marginalized youth in Sikkim.

Keepu began her career in 1967 as principal of Enchey Senior Secondary School in Gangtok, later moving to Government High School, where she taught Tibetan refugee children. She joined the Sikkim Civil Service in 1994, rising to joint secretary and serving as project director of the Rural Development Agency for 28 years. Social Contributions

In 1997, Keepu founded the Human Development Foundation of Sikkim (HDFS) with support from Swiss donors. The organization runs a school, Padma Odzer Choeling, for 400 underprivileged children, emphasizing education and Lepcha cultural preservation. Since 1989, she has mentored Lepcha children at her home, Lepcha Cottage, which now houses over 100 orphans. Her work focuses on empowering girls, women, and the elderly, revitalizing the Lepcha language and culture.
