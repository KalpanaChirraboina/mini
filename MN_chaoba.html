Early Life and Education

Born in 1895 in Uripok Sorbon Thingen Leikai, Imphal, Khwairakpam Chaoba Singh grew up in a culturally rich Manipuri environment. Educated in Imphal, he was deeply influenced by Sanskrit literature and Meitei traditions, which shaped his literary style. His early exposure to Manipuri flora, fauna, and oral narratives fostered his melancholic and elegiac worldview.

Literary Career

Chaoba began his literary career with works like Chhatra Macha (1923) and Kannaba Wa (1924). He founded the Manipuri Sahitya Sammelini with Dr. Lamabam Kamal Singh, Hijam Irabot, and Hijam Anganghal, promoting Meitei literature. His historical novel Lavangalata (1939) and poetry collection Thainagi Leirang (1946), including the elegy Awaba, marked him as a pioneer of modern Manipuri literature.
